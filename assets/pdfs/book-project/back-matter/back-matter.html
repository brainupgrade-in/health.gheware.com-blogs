Appendix A

Glossary of Diabetes Terms

Basal InsulinLong-acting insulin that provides background control throughout the day
Blood GlucoseThe amount of sugar (glucose) present in your blood, measured in mg/dL
Bolus InsulinFast-acting insulin taken before meals to cover the food you eat
CarbohydratesNutrients in food that directly raise blood sugar; found in grains, fruits, dairy
CGMContinuous Glucose Monitor — a device that tracks blood sugar 24/7
Dawn PhenomenonNatural rise in blood sugar early morning (4-8 AM) due to hormone release
DKADiabetic Ketoacidosis — dangerous condition of very high blood sugar + ketones
Fasting GlucoseBlood sugar measured after not eating for 8+ hours; normal is 70-100 mg/dL
Glycemic IndexScale (0-100) measuring how quickly a food raises blood sugar
Glycemic LoadGI adjusted for portion size; better measure of actual blood sugar impact
HbA1c3-month average of blood sugar levels; target is usually below 7%
HyperglycemiaHigh blood sugar, generally above 180 mg/dL
HypoglycemiaLow blood sugar, below 70 mg/dL — requires immediate treatment
Insulin ResistanceWhen cells don't respond well to insulin; main cause of Type 2 diabetes
KetonesChemicals produced when body burns fat for energy instead of glucose
MetforminMost commonly prescribed diabetes medication; reduces liver glucose production
Post-PrandialAfter a meal; post-prandial glucose is typically measured 2 hours after eating
Pre-DiabetesBlood sugar higher than normal but not diabetic; HbA1c 5.7-6.4%
Time in Range% of time glucose stays 70-180 mg/dL; goal is >70% for most diabetics
Type 2 DiabetesMost common form; body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't make enough

Appendix B

Emergency Quick Reference

🔴 LOW BLOOD SUGAR — Below 70 mg/dL

Symptoms: Shaking, sweating, dizziness, confusion, rapid heartbeat

The 15-15 Rule:
1. Eat 15g fast carbs (4 glucose tablets / ½ cup juice / 1 tbsp honey)
2. Wait 15 minutes
3. Recheck blood sugar
4. If still below 70, repeat
5. Once normal, eat a snack with protein

⚠️ KEY NUMBERS

Normal Fasting: 70-100 mg/dL
Pre-Diabetic: 100-125 mg/dL
Diabetic: 126+ mg/dL
Emergency Low: Below 54 mg/dL
Emergency High: Above 400 mg/dL

🔴 HIGH BLOOD SUGAR — Above 300 mg/dL

Symptoms: Extreme thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision

Immediate Steps:
1. Drink plenty of water
2. Take medication as prescribed
3. Do NOT eat more carbs
4. Check for ketones (urine strip) if above 300
5. Call doctor if not improving in 2-3 hours
