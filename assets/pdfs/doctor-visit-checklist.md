# Doctor Visit Preparation Checklist
## Your Complete Guide to Making Every Appointment Count

*A comprehensive checklist for people with diabetes to maximize their healthcare visits*

---

## ‚úÖ BEFORE YOUR VISIT (1-2 Weeks Ahead)

### üìã Gather Your Health Logs
- [ ] **Blood sugar readings** - Print or export last 2-4 weeks of glucose data
- [ ] **Blood pressure log** - If you monitor at home, bring 2 weeks of readings
- [ ] **Food diary** - Even a rough log helps identify patterns
- [ ] **Exercise log** - Types of activity, duration, frequency
- [ ] **Sleep patterns** - Note any changes or disturbances
- [ ] **Weight tracking** - Weekly measurements if available

### üíä Medication Review
- [ ] **Current medication list** - Include ALL medications (prescription, OTC, supplements)
- [ ] **Dosages and timing** - How much, how often, what time of day
- [ ] **Refill needs** - Note any medications running low
- [ ] **Side effects experienced** - Write down any new or ongoing issues
- [ ] **Medications from other doctors** - Don't forget specialists' prescriptions

### üìù Symptoms to Document
- [ ] **New symptoms** - When did they start? How often? How severe (1-10)?
- [ ] **Changed symptoms** - Anything getting better or worse?
- [ ] **Hypoglycemia episodes** - Dates, times, what you were doing, how you treated it
- [ ] **Hyperglycemia episodes** - Dates, readings, possible causes
- [ ] **Pain or discomfort** - Location, type (sharp, dull, burning), triggers
- [ ] **Foot problems** - Cuts, sores, numbness, tingling, color changes
- [ ] **Vision changes** - Blurry vision, floaters, difficulty reading
- [ ] **Mood changes** - Anxiety, depression, stress levels

### üß™ Pre-Appointment Lab Work
*Schedule these 3-7 days before your visit so results are ready:*
- [ ] **Fasting blood glucose** - Done in the morning before eating
- [ ] **HbA1c** - Your 3-month blood sugar average
- [ ] **Complete metabolic panel** - Kidney and liver function
- [ ] **Lipid panel** - Cholesterol and triglycerides
- [ ] **Urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio** - Kidney health check

## üìã QUESTIONS TO ASK YOUR DOCTOR

### About Your Diabetes Management
- [ ] "Is my current HbA1c at a healthy level for me? What should my goal be?"
- [ ] "Are my blood sugar targets (fasting and post-meal) still appropriate?"
- [ ] "Should I adjust my testing frequency based on my control?"
- [ ] "Am I at risk for hypoglycemia? How can I prevent it?"
- [ ] "Are there any new treatments I should consider?"

### About Medications
- [ ] "Are there any interactions between my current medications?"
- [ ] "What are the side effects I should watch for with [medication name]?"
- [ ] "Is there a generic version available to reduce costs?"
- [ ] "What happens if I miss a dose of [medication name]?"
- [ ] "Should any medications be adjusted based on my recent labs?"
- [ ] "Are any of my medications affecting my blood sugar?"

### About Diet & Lifestyle
- [ ] "What specific dietary changes would benefit me most?"
- [ ] "How many carbohydrates should I aim for per meal?"
- [ ] "What types of exercise are safe and beneficial for me?"
- [ ] "Are there any foods I should completely avoid?"
- [ ] "Can you refer me to a diabetes educator or dietitian?"

### About Complications & Prevention
- [ ] "What is my current risk for heart disease?"
- [ ] "How are my kidneys functioning? Do I need any changes?"
- [ ] "When should I schedule my next eye exam?"
- [ ] "Should I see a podiatrist for foot care?"
- [ ] "What screening tests am I due for this year?"
- [ ] "What warning signs should prompt me to call you immediately?"

### About Mental Health
- [ ] "I've been feeling [stressed/anxious/depressed] - can we discuss this?"
- [ ] "Are there support groups or counseling services you recommend?"
- [ ] "Could my mood be related to my diabetes or medications?"

## üî¨ TESTS TO REQUEST OR CONFIRM

### Essential Tests (Every 3-6 Months)
| Test | What It Measures | Your Goal |
|------|------------------|-----------|
| HbA1c | 3-month blood sugar average | Usually <7% (individualized) |
| Fasting Glucose | Morning blood sugar | 80-130 mg/dL |
| Blood Pressure | Cardiovascular health | <130/80 mmHg |
| Weight/BMI | Metabolic health | Discuss with doctor |

### Annual Tests (At Least Once Per Year)
| Test | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| Lipid Panel | Total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, triglycerides | Heart disease risk |
| Comprehensive Metabolic Panel | Kidney function (eGFR, creatinine) | Kidney health |
| Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio | Protein in urine | Early kidney damage |
| Dilated Eye Exam | Retina health | Diabetic retinopathy |
| Comprehensive Foot Exam | Nerve and circulation | Neuropathy, foot ulcers |
| Dental Exam | Gum health | Periodontal disease |

### Tests to Discuss If Applicable
- [ ] **Thyroid function (TSH)** - Especially for Type 1 or symptoms present
- [ ] **Vitamin B12** - If taking metformin long-term
- [ ] **Vitamin D** - Common deficiency in diabetes
- [ ] **Liver enzymes (ALT)** - Fatty liver screening
- [ ] **C-Peptide** - If diabetes type is uncertain
- [ ] **Ankle-Brachial Index** - If circulation concerns exist

## üó£Ô∏è TIPS FOR EFFECTIVE COMMUNICATION

### Before You Speak
- [ ] **Prioritize your concerns** - List your top 3 most important issues
- [ ] **Be specific** - "I had low blood sugar 4 times last week" vs "I sometimes feel shaky"
- [ ] **Bring a support person** - They can help remember information
- [ ] **Write questions down** - Don't rely on memory

### During the Appointment
- [ ] **Start with priorities** - Mention your biggest concerns first
- [ ] **Be honest** - About medication adherence, diet, challenges you face
- [ ] **Ask for clarification** - "Can you explain that in simpler terms?"
- [ ] **Take notes** - Or ask permission to record the conversation
- [ ] **Repeat back** - "So you're saying I should..." to confirm understanding
- [ ] **Ask "What's next?"** - Make sure you know the action plan

### If You Don't Understand
Use these phrases:
- "Can you explain what that means?"
- "Why is this test/medication important?"
- "What are my options?"
- "What would happen if I don't do this?"
- "Can you write that down for me?"

## ‚úÖ DURING YOUR VISIT

### Physical Examination Checklist
Ensure your doctor checks:
- [ ] **Blood pressure** - Sitting, both arms ideally
- [ ] **Weight** - Track changes over time
- [ ] **Foot exam** - Pulses, sensation, skin, nails
- [ ] **Injection sites** - If using insulin (check for lumps)
- [ ] **Skin check** - Diabetes-related skin conditions
- [ ] **Heart and lung sounds**
- [ ] **Neck/thyroid palpation**

### Information to Confirm
- [ ] **Reviewed all medications** - Including any recent changes
- [ ] **Discussed lab results** - Understand what each number means
- [ ] **Covered any new symptoms**
- [ ] **Addressed your prepared questions**
- [ ] **Discussed lifestyle factors** - Diet, exercise, stress, sleep

---

## üìù AFTER YOUR VISIT

### Immediate Follow-Up (Within 24-48 Hours)
- [ ] **Review your notes** - Fill in any gaps while memory is fresh
- [ ] **Pick up prescriptions** - New or refilled medications
- [ ] **Schedule recommended tests** - Lab work, imaging, specialist visits
- [ ] **Schedule follow-up appointment** - Don't leave without a date
- [ ] **Set up any referrals** - Specialists, dietitian, diabetes educator

### Action Items to Complete
- [ ] **Understand medication changes** - New doses, timing, or medications
- [ ] **Know your numbers** - Write down your current HbA1c, BP, cholesterol
- [ ] **Implement lifestyle changes** - Start any new diet/exercise recommendations
- [ ] **Update your health records** - Add new information to your personal file
- [ ] **Share information** - Update family members about your care plan

## üö® WHEN TO CALL YOUR DOCTOR IMMEDIATELY

Contact your healthcare provider right away if you experience:

- [ ] Blood sugar consistently above 300 mg/dL
- [ ] Blood sugar below 70 mg/dL that doesn't respond to treatment
- [ ] Symptoms of diabetic ketoacidosis: extreme thirst, nausea, vomiting, confusion, fruity breath
- [ ] Signs of infection: fever, redness, swelling, pus
- [ ] Foot wound that won't heal or shows signs of infection
- [ ] Sudden vision changes
- [ ] Chest pain or shortness of breath
- [ ] Severe headache or confusion
- [ ] Numbness or weakness on one side of the body
