## üíä Metformin (Glucophage, Glycomet, Glyciphage) {#metformin}

**How it works:** Reduces glucose production in the liver and improves insulin sensitivity

### Common Side Effects
| Side Effect | Frequency | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| Nausea | Very Common | Take with food |
| Diarrhea | Very Common | Start with low dose |
| Stomach upset | Common | Extended-release form may help |
| Metallic taste | Common | Usually improves over time |
| Loss of appetite | Common | May aid weight management |

### Serious Side Effects (Seek Immediate Help)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Lactic acidosis** (rare but serious): muscle pain, weakness, difficulty breathing, unusual tiredness
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Severe allergic reactions
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Vitamin B12 deficiency (long-term use)

### Key Points
- ‚úÖ First-line medication for Type 2 diabetes
- ‚úÖ Does NOT cause hypoglycemia when used alone
- ‚úÖ May help with weight management
- ‚ùå Avoid if kidney function is severely impaired
- ‚ùå Stop before CT scans with contrast dye

## üíä Sulfonylureas (Glimepiride, Glipizide, Gliclazide) {#sulfonylureas}

**Brand Names in India:** Amaryl, Glimisave, Glimy, Glimestar

**How it works:** Stimulates pancreas to release more insulin

### Common Side Effects
| Side Effect | Frequency | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)** | Common | Carry glucose tablets always |
| Weight gain | Common | Monitor diet and exercise |
| Nausea | Occasional | Take with breakfast |
| Dizziness | Occasional | Rise slowly from sitting |
| Skin rash | Rare | Report to doctor |

### Serious Side Effects (Seek Immediate Help)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Severe hypoglycemia**: confusion, sweating, shakiness, loss of consciousness
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Allergic reactions (especially if allergic to sulfa drugs)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes)

### Key Points
- ‚úÖ Effective and affordable
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **HIGH RISK of hypoglycemia** - never skip meals
- ‚ùå Avoid alcohol (increases hypoglycemia risk)
- ‚ùå Elderly patients need lower doses

## üíä DPP-4 Inhibitors (Sitagliptin, Vildagliptin, Linagliptin) {#dpp-4-inhibitors}

**Brand Names in India:** Januvia, Galvus, Trajenta, Zita, Jalra

**How it works:** Helps body produce more insulin when needed and reduces glucose production

### Common Side Effects
| Side Effect | Frequency | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| Headache | Common | Usually mild, resolves |
| Upper respiratory infection | Common | Practice good hygiene |
| Stuffy/runny nose | Common | Symptomatic treatment |
| Sore throat | Occasional | Stay hydrated |
| Joint pain | Occasional | Report if persistent |

### Serious Side Effects (Seek Immediate Help)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Pancreatitis**: severe stomach pain radiating to back, nausea, vomiting
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Severe joint pain
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Allergic reactions: skin blisters, rash
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Heart failure symptoms (shortness of breath, swelling)

### Key Points
- ‚úÖ Low risk of hypoglycemia
- ‚úÖ Weight neutral
- ‚úÖ Once daily dosing
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Report any severe stomach pain immediately

## üíä SGLT2 Inhibitors (Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin, Canagliflozin) {#sglt2-inhibitors}

**Brand Names in India:** Forxiga, Jardiance, Invokana, Glyxambi

**How it works:** Removes excess glucose through urine

### Common Side Effects
| Side Effect | Frequency | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Urinary tract infections** | Common | Drink plenty of water, good hygiene |
| **Genital yeast infections** | Common | Keep area dry, antifungal cream |
| Increased urination | Very Common | Expected effect, stay hydrated |
| Thirst | Common | Drink adequate water |
| Low blood pressure | Occasional | Rise slowly, stay hydrated |

### Serious Side Effects (Seek Immediate Help)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)**: nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, confusion, unusual fatigue
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Fournier's gangrene** (rare): pain, tenderness, redness in genital area
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Severe dehydration
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Kidney problems

### Key Points
- ‚úÖ Promotes weight loss (2-3 kg typically)
- ‚úÖ Lowers blood pressure
- ‚úÖ **Heart and kidney protective benefits**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Stop before surgery (risk of DKA)
- ‚ùå Not recommended if frequent UTIs
- ‚ùå Maintain good genital hygiene

## üíä GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (Liraglutide, Semaglutide, Dulaglutide) {#glp-1-receptor-agonists}

**Brand Names in India:** Victoza, Ozempic, Rybelsus, Trulicity

**How it works:** Mimics natural hormone to increase insulin, reduce appetite, slow digestion

### Common Side Effects
| Side Effect | Frequency | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Nausea** | Very Common | Start low, increase slowly |
| **Vomiting** | Common | Eat smaller meals |
| Diarrhea | Common | Usually improves in weeks |
| Constipation | Common | Increase fiber and water |
| Decreased appetite | Common | This aids weight loss |
| Injection site reactions | Common | Rotate injection sites |

### Serious Side Effects (Seek Immediate Help)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Pancreatitis**: severe stomach pain, nausea, vomiting
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Thyroid tumors** (seen in animal studies): lump in neck, hoarseness, difficulty swallowing
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Gallbladder problems
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Kidney problems
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Severe allergic reactions

### Key Points
- ‚úÖ **Significant weight loss** (often 5-15% body weight)
- ‚úÖ Heart protective benefits
- ‚úÖ Once weekly options available
- ‚ö†Ô∏è GI side effects usually improve over 4-8 weeks
- ‚ùå Not for patients with history of thyroid cancer
- ‚ùå Not for patients with MEN 2 syndrome

## üíä Insulin Types {#insulin-types}

### Rapid-Acting Insulin (Humalog, NovoRapid, Apidra)
| Side Effect | Frequency | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Hypoglycemia** | Common | Time with meals, monitor glucose |
| Weight gain | Common | Adjust diet accordingly |
| Injection site reactions | Common | Rotate sites |
| Lipodystrophy | With poor rotation | Rotate injection sites |

### Short-Acting Insulin (Regular/Actrapid)
- Similar to rapid-acting but slower onset
- Take 30 minutes before meals

### Intermediate-Acting Insulin (NPH/Humulin N)
| Side Effect | Frequency | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Hypoglycemia** (especially at night) | Common | Bedtime snack may help |
| Weight gain | Common | Monitor diet |
| Variable absorption | Common | Mix well before injecting |

### Long-Acting Insulin (Lantus, Levemir, Tresiba)
| Side Effect | Frequency | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| Hypoglycemia (less common) | Occasional | Monitor, adjust dose |
| Weight gain | Common | Diet and exercise |
| Injection site reactions | Occasional | Rotate sites |

### Key Points for All Insulin
- ‚úÖ Most effective glucose-lowering medication
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Always carry fast-acting glucose** (glucose tablets, juice)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Never skip meals after taking insulin
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Teach family members about hypoglycemia treatment
- ‚ùå Store properly (refrigerate unopened, room temp once opened)

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Drug Interactions to Watch For {#drug-interactions}

### Medications That INCREASE Hypoglycemia Risk
| Interacting Drug | Affected Diabetes Meds | Action |
|-----------------|----------------------|--------|
| **Alcohol** | All, especially Sulfonylureas | Limit alcohol, never drink on empty stomach |
| **Beta-blockers** (Atenolol, Metoprolol) | All | May mask hypoglycemia symptoms |
| **ACE inhibitors** (Enalapril, Ramipril) | All | Monitor blood sugar more frequently |
| **Aspirin** (high doses) | Sulfonylureas | Monitor for low blood sugar |
| **Warfarin** | Sulfonylureas | Increased bleeding risk, monitor INR |

### Medications That DECREASE Diabetes Drug Effectiveness
| Interacting Drug | Effect | Action |
|-----------------|--------|--------|
| **Steroids** (Prednisolone) | Raises blood sugar significantly | May need dose adjustment |
| **Thiazide diuretics** | Raises blood sugar | Monitor glucose |
| **Thyroid medications** | Can affect glucose levels | Regular monitoring |

### Specific Interactions by Drug Class

**Metformin:**
- ‚ùå Avoid with IV contrast dye (stop 48 hours before/after)
- ‚ùå Limit alcohol (lactic acidosis risk)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Caution with kidney-affecting drugs

**SGLT2 Inhibitors:**
- ‚ùå Caution with diuretics (dehydration risk)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è May enhance blood pressure medications

**GLP-1 Agonists:**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è May slow absorption of oral medications
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Take oral meds 1 hour before GLP-1

## üö® When to Contact Your Doctor IMMEDIATELY {#when-to-contact-your-doctor}

### Emergency Symptoms (Call Doctor or Go to Hospital)
- üî¥ Blood sugar below 70 mg/dL that doesn't respond to treatment
- üî¥ Blood sugar above 300 mg/dL consistently
- üî¥ Confusion, difficulty speaking, or loss of consciousness
- üî¥ Severe stomach pain with nausea/vomiting (possible pancreatitis)
- üî¥ Difficulty breathing, rapid breathing
- üî¥ Chest pain or pressure
- üî¥ Fruity-smelling breath (sign of DKA)
- üî¥ Severe infection signs: fever, redness, swelling
- üî¥ Signs of severe allergic reaction: swelling of face/throat, difficulty breathing

### Contact Within 24-48 Hours
- üü° Persistent diarrhea or vomiting (more than 24 hours)
- üü° Repeated episodes of low blood sugar
- üü° New skin rash or itching
- üü° Unusual fatigue or weakness
- üü° Painful or frequent urination (possible UTI)
- üü° Genital itching or discharge (possible yeast infection)
- üü° Joint pain that is new or worsening
- üü° Swelling in feet or ankles

### Discuss at Next Visit
- üü¢ Mild stomach upset that doesn't improve
- üü¢ Gradual weight changes
- üü¢ Difficulty remembering to take medication
- üü¢ Questions about medication timing or dosing

## üí° Tips to Minimize Side Effects {#tips-to-minimize-side-effects}

### General Tips for All Medications
1. **Take medications as prescribed** - same time each day
2. **Never skip doses** without consulting your doctor
3. **Stay hydrated** - drink 8-10 glasses of water daily
4. **Keep a medication diary** - track side effects and blood sugar
5. **Store medications properly** - check temperature requirements

### Metformin Tips
- ‚úÖ Take with food to reduce stomach upset
- ‚úÖ Start with low dose and increase gradually
- ‚úÖ Ask about extended-release formulation
- ‚úÖ Have B12 levels checked annually

### Sulfonylurea Tips
- ‚úÖ **Never skip meals** - eat regularly
- ‚úÖ Always carry glucose tablets or candy
- ‚úÖ Take with breakfast (once daily forms)
- ‚úÖ Avoid alcohol, especially on empty stomach
- ‚úÖ Teach family about hypoglycemia symptoms

### SGLT2 Inhibitor Tips
- ‚úÖ Drink plenty of water (2-3 liters daily)
- ‚úÖ Practice excellent genital hygiene
- ‚úÖ Urinate after sexual activity
- ‚úÖ Wear cotton underwear
- ‚úÖ Don't take if you're dehydrated or sick

### GLP-1 Agonist Tips
- ‚úÖ Start with lowest dose for 4 weeks
- ‚úÖ Eat smaller, more frequent meals
- ‚úÖ Avoid fatty or spicy foods initially
- ‚úÖ Rotate injection sites
- ‚úÖ Be patient - nausea usually improves in 4-8 weeks

### Insulin Tips
- ‚úÖ Rotate injection sites systematically
- ‚úÖ Don't inject into same spot repeatedly
- ‚úÖ Check expiration dates
- ‚úÖ Inspect insulin before use (no particles or discoloration)
- ‚úÖ Keep fast-acting glucose everywhere (home, car, office)

### Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar) - The Rule of 15
1. **Check** blood sugar if below 70 mg/dL
2. **Eat** 15 grams of fast-acting carbs:
   - 3-4 glucose tablets
   - ¬Ω cup fruit juice
   - 1 tablespoon honey
   - 5-6 hard candies
3. **Wait** 15 minutes
4. **Recheck** blood sugar
5. **Repeat** if still below 70 mg/dL

### Hyperglycemia (High Blood Sugar) - Warning Signs
- Increased thirst and urination
- Blurred vision
- Fatigue
- Fruity-smelling breath (emergency!)

### Emergency Contacts
- Keep your doctor's number saved
- Know nearest hospital with diabetic emergency care
- Wear medical ID bracelet
